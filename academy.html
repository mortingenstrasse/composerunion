THE BLUES & JAZZ TRADITION

Blues and jazz piano represent some of the most influential music in history. Born in the late 19th and early 20th centuries, these genres revolutionized Western music and continue to influence virtually every popular style today.

The New Orleans Legacy

New Orleans, the birthplace of jazz, gave the world a unique musical language that combined African rhythms, European harmony, and Caribbean influences. The city's legendary piano players – from Jelly Roll Morton to Professor Longhair – created a distinctive style characterized by rolling left-hand patterns, syncopated rhythms, and joyful improvisation. The New Orleans piano tradition emphasizes the groove – that infectious rhythmic feel that makes people want to move. This same spirit infuses our teaching approach, ensuring that you don't just learn notes, but learn to make music that swings, grooves, and communicates emotion.

The Masters Who Shaped the Genre

Our curriculum draws inspiration from the greatest jazz and blues pianists in history. You'll learn techniques pioneered by legends like Thelonious Monk, whose angular melodies and distinctive chord voicings redefined jazz harmony; Oscar Peterson, whose virtuosic technique and swinging style set the standard for modern jazz piano; Bill Evans, who brought impressionistic harmony and introspective beauty to jazz; and Art Tatum, whose incredible technical facility and harmonic sophistication remain unmatched. Blues piano masters like Otis Spann, Pinetop Perkins, and Memphis Slim will guide our exploration of authentic blues styles, teaching you the raw, emotional power of the 12-bar blues and the art of playing with soul and conviction.

COMPLETE COURSE CURRICULUM

Our program is structured into three progressive levels, each building upon the previous foundation. Content is continuously updated with new songs, exercises, and techniques to keep your learning fresh and engaging.

BEGINNER LEVEL: FOUNDATION BUILDING

Duration: Weeks 1-12 | Focus: Essential Skills & Blues Basics

If you're new to piano or returning after years away, this is where your journey begins. We'll start from the absolute basics and build a rock-solid foundation.

What You'll Learn:

  • Piano Fundamentals: Proper posture, hand position, finger numbering, and keyboard geography
  • Note Reading: Treble and bass clef notation, rhythm values, time signatures
  • Major and Minor Scales: All 12 major scales and their relative minors, with proper fingering
  • Basic Chords: Triads (major, minor, diminished, augmented) in root position
  • The 12-Bar Blues: Understanding and playing the fundamental blues progression in multiple keys
  • Blues Scale: Learning the blues scale and using it for simple improvisation
  • Simple Left-Hand Patterns: Root-fifth patterns, octave patterns, and basic walking bass lines
  • Rhythm & Timing: Playing with a metronome, understanding swing feel, and basic syncopation
  • First Songs: Classic blues tunes like "C Jam Blues," "Blue Monk," and simple boogie-woogie patterns

Practice Philosophy: At this level, repetition is everything. You'll practice each scale, chord, and pattern dozens of times until it becomes automatic. Our software will guide you through each repetition, ensuring perfect accuracy.

INTERMEDIATE LEVEL: EXPANDING YOUR VOCABULARY

Duration: Weeks 13-32 | Focus: Jazz Harmony & Advanced Blues

With your foundation secure, we now expand your harmonic vocabulary and introduce authentic jazz and blues techniques used by professional musicians.

What You'll Learn:

  • Seventh Chords: Dominant 7th, major 7th, minor 7th, half-diminished, and diminished 7th chords
  • Chord Inversions: Playing chords in all inversions for smoother voice leading
  • Extended Chords: Introduction to 9th, 11th, and 13th chords
  • Walking Bass Lines: Creating authentic swinging bass lines that move through chord changes
  • Stride Piano: The left-hand technique that defined early jazz piano
  • Blues Variations: Minor blues, jazz blues, and contemporary blues forms
  • Comping Patterns: Rhythmic chord accompaniment patterns used in jazz
  • II-V-I Progressions: The most important progression in jazz, in all keys
  • Modal Playing: Understanding and using church modes (Dorian, Mixolydian, etc.)
  • Intermediate Repertoire: Jazz standards like "Autumn Leaves," "Blue Bossa," classic blues tunes, and more complex arrangements
  • Basic Improvisation: Soloing over blues and simple jazz progressions using scales and chord tones

Practice Philosophy: Now we focus on musical expression alongside technical precision. You'll learn to play with dynamics, phrasing, and swing feel while continuing to drill the fundamentals through varied repetition.

ADVANCED LEVEL: PROFESSIONAL ARTISTRY

Duration: Weeks 33+ | Focus: Mastery & Personal Voice

At this level, you're ready to explore the full depth of jazz harmony, advanced improvisation, and sophisticated repertoire. This is where you develop your unique artistic voice.

What You'll Learn:

  • Advanced Jazz Voicings: Rootless voicings, upper structure triads, quartal harmony, cluster chords
  • Reharmonization: Substituting and altering chord progressions for richer harmonic color
  • Complex Rhythms: Polyrhythms, odd time signatures, and rhythmic displacement
  • Advanced Walking Bass: Chromatic approaches, scalar connections, and harmonic anticipation
  • Modern Jazz Concepts: Contemporary harmony used by current jazz artists
  • Advanced Improvisation: Melodic development, motivic improvisation, playing outside, and advanced scale choices
  • Arrangement Skills: Creating your own arrangements of standards and original compositions
  • Style Studies: Deep dives into the styles of specific pianists and eras
  • Advanced Repertoire: Complex jazz standards, bebop tunes, modal jazz, and contemporary compositions
  • Performance Skills: Playing with confidence, stage presence, and communicating with other musicians

Practice Philosophy: Advanced practice focuses on refinement, creativity, and artistic interpretation. You'll learn to practice like a professional – with specific goals, analytical listening, and creative exploration of the material.

THE POWER OF REPETITION & STRUCTURED PRACTICE

There's an old joke among musicians: "How do you get to Carnegie Hall? Practice, practice, practice!" While it may be a cliché, it contains an essential truth. The difference between good pianists and great pianists isn't talent – it's the quality and quantity of their practice. Our program is built on the principle of intelligent repetition. This means:

  • Focused Practice Sessions: Each lesson includes specific exercises designed to be repeated until they become second nature
  • Progressive Difficulty: We gradually increase complexity, ensuring you're always challenged but never overwhelmed
  • Visual Feedback: Our software shows you exactly what to play, eliminating guesswork and building confidence
  • Measurable Progress: Track your improvement through recorded practice sessions and periodic assessments
  • Varied Repetition: Practice the same concept in different keys, tempos, and musical contexts to ensure deep understanding

Remember: Practice doesn't make perfect – perfect practice makes perfect. By following our structured approach and committing to regular, focused practice sessions, you'll develop skills that last a lifetime.
